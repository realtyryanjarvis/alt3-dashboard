## The Sales Arc

**Open → Context → Why It Matters → What We Build → The Experience → Your Strategy → The Offer → How It Works → Close**

The first half builds the case for why intelligence matters in this market right now.
The second half shows them exactly what you're offering and how to work together.
Don't rush. Each slide earns the next one.

## Slide-by-Slide Presenter Guide

---

### SLIDE 1 — Hero
**"The AI Home Era Is Here."**

> "I'm not here to sell you on smart home technology. I'm here to show you where your buyers already are — and what that means for how you build."

Let it breathe. Don't rush off this slide.

### SLIDE 2 — We Are Here / Evolution Timeline
**1950s → 1970s → 2000s → 2010s → Now**

> "Every era of home building had a defining technology that separated the builders who led from the ones who followed. Central air. Dishwashers. High-speed internet. Every single time, the builders who moved first owned the market."

Point to the 'You Are Here' navy column:
> "This isn't coming. It's already here. And your buyers are already living in it — AI in their pocket, AI in their car, AI at their desk. They expect it in their home."

### SLIDE 3 — The Opportunity
**87% · 3–5% · Now**

> "87% of luxury buyers say they won't consider a home that isn't tech-friendly. That's not a preference — that's a filter. And homes with professionally integrated systems are selling for 3 to 5 percent more at closing. On a $2 million home, that's $60,000 to $100,000 sitting on the table."

Point to the third card:
> "Most builders in this market haven't made this move yet. That's your window — and it won't stay open forever."

### SLIDE 4 — The Buyer Reality
**72% · 61% · 2×**

> "72% of buyers want the technology already installed when they move in. They don't want to manage contractors after closing. And this one really stands out — smart homes sell for nearly double the price of non-smart homes. That's Vivint's 2025 research across real estate listings nationwide."

The hook:
> "These buyers are going to spend this money regardless. The question is whether they spend it in your home — or six months after they move in, with someone else."

### SLIDE 9 — Your Play
**Pre-wire warning + two paths**

Now they've seen the product. The strategy makes sense.

> "Here's where most builders are right now — they pre-wire and call it done. In luxury, that's not enough anymore. Buyers expect the experience, not the infrastructure. The bar has moved."

> "But that actually works in your favor. Because it creates two ways to win."

Walk through both cards:
> "If you presale — we bundle a full package before closing. Buyer walks in, everything works. You command a premium, close faster, deliver something no competing home can match."

> "If it sells at close — the wiring is already in. We come in after closing, activate the system, zero disruption. The home was built for this — and buyers recognize it."

Close with the bridge:
> "The question isn't whether to do this. It's which level fits your build."

**"Our buyers don't ask for this."**
> "87% of luxury buyers say they won't consider a home that isn't tech-friendly. They may not say it to you — but they're saying it to their agent."

> "Pre-wire is the floor now, not the ceiling. Buyers expect the experience — not the promise of one. That's exactly why we exist."
